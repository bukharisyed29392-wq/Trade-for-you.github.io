What is Stock Trading?

Stock trading involves buying and selling shares of companies on the stock market. Stocks represent ownership in a company, and their value can rise or fall based on the company's performance and market conditions.

Basic Concepts

  • Stocks: Shares of ownership in a company. Buying stocks means you own a piece of that company.
  • Stock Market: A marketplace (like NYSE or NASDAQ) where stocks are traded.
  • Investing vs. Trading: Investing is long-term (holding stocks for years), while trading is short-term (buying/selling frequently to profit from price changes).
  • Risk: Stocks can lose value, so diversification (spreading investments) is key.

How to Get Started

  1. Open a brokerage account (e.g., with apps like Robinhood or Fidelity).
  2. Research stocks: Look at company performance, financials, and news.
  3. Start small: Invest what you can afford to lose, even $25 a week.
  4. Learn tools: Use charts, apps, and free resources like Investopedia.

Tips for Beginners

Start with education, avoid emotional decisions, and practice with paper trading (simulated trades). Remember, past performance doesn't guarantee future results.
